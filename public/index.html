Theory Base

Analytics Foundations

Understand the core loop: business question -> metric design -> data collection -> analysis -> decision -> outcome tracking.

  • Descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive analytics.
  • North Star metric design and metric trees.
  • Leading vs lagging indicators and confidence in decisions.

Example: For subscription products, activation rate is often a leading indicator for 30-day retention.

Decision Science

Statistics for Product Analytics

Use statistics to decide under uncertainty, not just to report averages.

  • Sampling, distributions, confidence intervals, and hypothesis testing.
  • A/B test design: sample size, power, guardrail metrics, segmentation.
  • Causal pitfalls: Simpson's paradox, survivorship bias, and p-hacking.

Best practice: Pre-register experiment success criteria before launch.

Builder Toolkit

SQL Mastery Path

SQL is still the highest-leverage skill for analysts and growth teams.

  • Core querying: joins, CTEs, window functions, and date handling.
  • Analytics patterns: cohort retention, funnel drop-off, customer LTV.
  • Performance mindset: indexing concepts and query cost awareness.

Project: Build a weekly KPI mart with one trusted query per metric.

Mindset

Analytics Skills & Mindset

Great analysts pair technical fluency with structured thinking and clear storytelling.

  • Problem framing, decomposition, and hypothesis-driven analysis.
  • Stakeholder alignment: convert ambiguity into measurable outcomes.
  • Decision memos: recommendation, tradeoffs, risk, and next test.

Real world pattern: Teams trust analysts who expose assumptions and uncertainty clearly.

Reliability

Data Governance & Quality

Reliable decisions require trustworthy data contracts and clear ownership.

  • Ownership model: domain stewards, metric owners, freshness SLA.
  • Data quality checks: null rates, duplicates, schema drift, anomaly alerts.
  • Privacy basics: retention policy, access controls, and auditability.

Case: A canonical "active customer" metric removes cross-team reporting conflict.

Pipelines

Data Engineering for Analytics

Modern analytics needs robust pipelines, observability, and reproducible transformations.

  • Batch vs streaming architecture and late-arriving event handling.
  • ELT modeling in layers: staging, intermediate, mart.
  • Pipeline health: lineage, test coverage, and backfill strategy.

Project: Design an event pipeline supporting both product funnels and finance reporting.

Playbooks

Analytics Frameworks

Frameworks reduce chaos and make analysis outputs reusable.

  • AARRR funnel for growth diagnostics.
  • HEART for product health and user experience metrics.
  • Opportunity Solution Tree for linking user problems to outcomes.

Best practice: Pair every framework with a metric dictionary and review cadence.

Growth Engine

SaaS Growth Analytics

SaaS growth compounds when acquisition quality, activation, retention, and monetization align.

  • Core metrics: MRR, ARR, CAC, payback period, NRR, gross/net churn.
  • Pricing & packaging analysis for expansion revenue.
  • Lifecycle analytics from signup to expansion loops.

Case study: Improving onboarding completion by 8% lifted 90-day retention and NRR.
